★ Long Island’s Path to Independence

The Long Island 250th Passport

To mark 250 years since the signing of the Declaration of Independence, the Suffolk County 250th committee created a commemorative Revolutionary Historical Passport — a booklet that turns Long Island’s Revolutionary War history into a stamp-collecting adventure. The sites span the whole island, Suffolk and Nassau alike. Long Island was no bystander: from the Culper Spy Ring of Setauket that fed intelligence to General Washington, to the British-occupied South Shore, these 100+ sites each played a part in the nation’s founding.

Carry the passport to participating museums, historic homes, and landmarks. At each one you collect a unique stamp — building a personal record of your journey through the county’s Revolutionary past. It’s built for families, students, and history buffs alike.

★ How to get a passport book
  • Free PDF — download and print at home from suffolkcountyny.gov.
  • Printed copy — order online from Amazon, Barnes & Noble Online, or Lulu.com (B&N online only, not in stores).
  • A limited number of paperbacks are also sold at some participating sites — ask when you visit.
